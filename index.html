Bees transfer pollen, helping plants reproduce and contributing to about 75% of global food production.

Bees are declining due to pesticides, habitat loss, and climate change.

The loss of bees reduces crop yields and disrupts ecosystems, threatening our food supply and environmental balance.

Problem Statement

The global bee population is experiencing a dramatic decline due to factors such as habitat loss, pesticide use, climate change, and diseases. This issue affects not only farmers and industries that rely on agriculture but also the entire ecosystem. Bees are key pollinators for plants, and their decline can lead to reduced crop production and threats to biodiversity. Particularly, a significant portion of the food consumed by humans is pollinated by bees, so their extinction poses serious risks to food security.
